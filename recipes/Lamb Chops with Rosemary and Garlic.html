Lamb Chops with Rosemary and Garlic

How to make the best Lamb Chops

Herb-Crusted Lamb Chops with Rosemary & Garlic Pan-seared to succulent, tender perfection, these premium lamb chops feature a deeply caramelized crust infused with aromatic fresh rosemary, crushed garlic, and a touch of cracked black pepper. Basted in rich, foaming butter and finished with a bright squeeze of fresh lemon, it is an effortlessly elegant, savory masterpiece that melts in your mouth.


Ingredients

  • Lamb Chops
  • Fresh Rosemary
  • Garlic
  • Olive Oil
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Coarse Sea Salt & Freshly Cracked Black Pepper
  • Lemon Wedges

Preparation

  1. Bring to Room Temp: Take the lamb chops out of the refrigerator 15 to 20 minutes before cooking. Pat them completely dry with a paper towel (moisture prevents a good sear).
  2. Season Generously: Rub the lamb chops with a splash of olive oil, then press the finely chopped fresh rosemary, minced garlic, coarse sea salt, and cracked black pepper firmly onto both sides of the meat.
  3. Place a heavy skillet (cast iron works best) over medium-high heat.
  4. Add a tablespoon of olive oil. Wait until the oil is shimmering and just starting to faintly smoke.
  5. Lay them down: Place the lamb chops in the hot skillet. If they have a thick strip of fat on the side, use tongs to hold them upright on that fat edge for 1 to 2 minutes to render it down and get it crispy.
  6. Sear the sides: Lay them flat and cook without moving them for 3 to 4 minutes until a deep, golden-brown crust forms.
  7. Flip: Flip the chops over to sear the other side for another 2 to 3 minutes.
  8. Turn the heat down to medium.
  9. Toss the unsalted butter and a few whole sprigs of rosemary into the pan.
  10. As the butter melts and foams up, tilt the skillet slightly and use a large spoon to continuously scoop the hot, herb-infused butter over the top of the lamb chops for about 1 minute.
  11. Check the Doneness: For a perfect medium-rare, the internal temperature should reach 57°C (135°F).
  12. Rest: Remove the lamb chops from the pan and place them on a warm plate. Tent them loosely with aluminum foil and let them rest for 5 minutes. Do not skip this—resting allows the juices to redistribute back into the meat.
  13. Finish: Plate the chops, pour a little of the remaining pan butter over them, and serve with a squeeze of fresh lemon juice.
