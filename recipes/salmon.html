Pan-Seared Salmon with Garlic-Herb Butter

How to make the best salmon

Experience a flawless contrast of textures with this premium, center-cut salmon fillet. Sear-crisped to a beautiful golden exterior while remaining incredibly tender, flaky, and buttery on the inside, it is finished in a luxurious, pooling pan-sauce of melted butter, fragrant minced garlic, and fresh garden herbs. A final, bright splash of lemon juice cuts through the richness, creating a perfectly balanced, restaurant-quality seafood dish.


Ingredients

  • Salmon Fillets
  • Salt & Black Pepper
  • Garlic Powder & Paprika
  • Olive Oil
  • Unsalted Butter
  • Lemon
  • Fresh Garlic
  • Fresh Dill or Parsley

Preparation

  1. Bring to Room Temp: Take the salmon fillets out of the refrigerator about 10 to 15 minutes before cooking to take the chill off.
  2. Dry Thoroughly: Pat the salmon fillets completely dry on both sides with a paper towel. Moisture is the enemy of a crispy sear!
  3. Season: Lightly dust the flesh side of the salmon with garlic powder, paprika, salt, and black pepper. Season the skin side with just a pinch of salt.
  4. Once the oil is shimmering and hot, carefully place the salmon fillets in the pan, skin-side down , use a flexible spatula to gently press down on each fillet for about 10 seconds. Salmon tends to curl up when it hits a hot pan; pressing keeps it flat so the skin crisps evenly.
  5. Hold Steady: Let it cook undisturbed for 4 to 5 minutes. You will see the color of the flesh slowly turn opaque, moving from the bottom of the fillet upward.
  6. Carefully flip the fillets over to the flesh side. Turn the heat down to medium.
  7. Immediately drop the unsalted butter, minced garlic, and a few lemon slices into the pan right next to the salmon.
  8. As the butter melts and foams up with the garlic, tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to continuously scoop that hot garlic butter over the crispy skin for about 1 to 2 minutes.
  9. Remove from Heat: The salmon is done when it feels slightly firm to the touch and flakes easily with a fork.
  10. Plate: Transfer the fillets to plates, skin-side up to preserve that beautiful crunch.
  11. Finish: Spoon the remaining garlic butter from the pan over the top, squeeze a bit of fresh lemon juice over everything, and garnish with fresh dill or parsley.
