Cheesecake Recipe

How to make a cheesecake

Rich, velvety, and undeniably decadent. This classic New York-style cheesecake features a dense yet incredibly smooth cream cheese filling baked over a buttery, crisp graham cracker crust. Balanced with a hint of fresh lemon zest and warm vanilla, it’s a timeless dessert that strikes the perfect balance between sweet, tangy, and deeply satisfying.


Ingredients

  • Graham cracker crumbs
  • Sour cream
  • Vanilla
  • Eggs
  • Sugar
  • Lemon
  • Cream cheese
  • Butter

Preparation

  1. Combine: In a medium bowl, mix the graham cracker crumbs with a couple of tablespoons of sugar (to help bind it) and melted butter until the mixture feels like wet sand.
  2. Press: Pour the mixture into a 9-inch springform pan. Use the bottom of a flat glass or a measuring cup to press the crumbs firmly and evenly into the bottom and slightly up the sides of the pan.
  3. Bake & Cool: Bake the crust for 10 minutes, then remove it from the oven and let it cool completely while you make the filling.
  4. Beat the Cheese: In a large bowl, beat the room-temperature cream cheese and sugar together on medium speed for about 2 to 3 minutes. Stop and scrape down the sides of the bowl to ensure it is completely smooth and fluffy.
  5. Add Wet Ingredients: Mix in the sour cream, vanilla extract, a squeeze of fresh lemon juice, and a little lemon zest for brightness. Beat on low speed just until combined.
  6. Add Eggs Last: Reduce the mixer to the lowest speed. Add the eggs one at a time, mixing just until the yellow disappears.
